Who We Are

US education system is quite similar to that in other countries. It consists of three main stages: primary education (kindergarten and elementary school), secondary education (middle school and high school), and further education (tertiary or higher education). Compulsory education in the country includes elementary school, middle school, and high school education. It is for children from the age of 5 to the age of 18. The first year of compulsory education is associated with the elementary school, also known in the US as primary school, grade school or grammar school. Elementary education is followed by secondary education. The secondary school in the US is often divided into junior high school (middle school) and senior high school (high school).

Everyone who wants to study in the US can choose from a wide number of educational institutions to find the perfect one. There are universities with small campuses that feel like home, as well as there are large ones that feel like cities. You can study in such world-famous places and cities as Las Vegas, Manhattan, the Grand Canyon, whatever. The US is ready to offer something for every taste and every type of learner.

Training

The popularity of the US among international students is a key to its diversity. Classes in the country consist of students of all ages, nationalities, and religions. Such culturally diverse learning environment promotes cross-cultural understanding and networking. Furthermore, each student in the US can enjoy a very active social life that includes various clubs and organizations for students, campus life, visiting museums, libraries and art galleries, as well as traveling around the big country..

Internship

International students in the US are allowed to work up to 20 hours a week. There is a Curricular Practical Training for those who want to work for university credit in a job in a related field. All the institutions in the country have a career services department which helps students to find a job during their study, as well as after the study. Furthermore, each student can take part in numerous extracurricular activities to get a job experience and to hone what was learned in class. For example, students can be involved in writing for the school newspaper, participating in science fairs, joining the debate team, etc

Research

The USA provides students with education in the “liberal arts”. Though it is not considered to be a specific major, it provides you with a benefit to get prepared for more than just immediate career plan. Liberal arts are focused on making you smarter, more thoughtful and more successful person. As the result, you can easily succeed in a job of your major. Also, liberal arts allow you to choose your own career path, whether you want to become a manager, to start your own business, providing you with limitless possibilities upon graduation.
